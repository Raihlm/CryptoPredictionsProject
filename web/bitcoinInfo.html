What is Bitcoin?

Understanding the world's first cryptocurrency

Digital Currency

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency that enables peer-to-peer transactions without the need for intermediaries like banks. It exists only in digital form and is secured by cryptographic principles.

Blockchain Technology

Built on blockchain technology, Bitcoin maintains a public ledger of all transactions. This distributed database is maintained by a network of computers worldwide, ensuring transparency and security.

Decentralized & Secure

No single authority controls Bitcoin. The network is maintained by thousands of nodes worldwide, making it resistant to censorship and single points of failure while ensuring maximum security.

The Mystery Creator

Satoshi Nakamoto - The pseudonymous genius behind Bitcoin

Satoshi Nakamoto

Satoshi Nakamoto is the pseudonymous person or group who created Bitcoin and authored its original white paper in 2008. Despite numerous investigations and theories, Satoshi's true identity remains one of the biggest mysteries in the tech world.

Key Facts:

  • Published the Bitcoin whitepaper on October 31, 2008
  • Released the first Bitcoin software in January 2009
  • Gradually faded from public view around 2011
  • Estimated to own around 1 million bitcoins (never moved)
  • Communicated only through online forums and emails

Satoshi's disappearance was intentional, ensuring Bitcoin could evolve as a truly decentralized system without a central figurehead. This selfless act helped establish Bitcoin's credibility as a peer-to-peer currency.

How Bitcoin Works

Understanding the technology behind digital gold

Transactions

When you send Bitcoin, you're broadcasting a transaction to the network. This transaction includes the sender's address, receiver's address, amount, and a digital signature proving ownership of the funds.

Blockchain

Transactions are grouped into blocks and linked together chronologically, forming a chain. Each block contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block, creating an immutable record.

Consensus

The network uses Proof of Work consensus mechanism. Miners compete to solve complex mathematical puzzles to validate transactions and add new blocks to the blockchain.

Bitcoin Mining

The process that secures the network and creates new bitcoins

1

Transaction Collection

Miners collect pending transactions from the network's memory pool (mempool) and verify their validity.
2

Hash Calculation

Miners use powerful computers to solve complex mathematical puzzles, trying billions of calculations per second.
3

Block Reward

The first miner to solve the puzzle broadcasts the solution and receives newly created bitcoins plus transaction fees.
4

Network Update

Other nodes verify the solution and add the new block to their copy of the blockchain, and the process repeats.

Bitcoin Timeline

Key milestones in Bitcoin's history

Bitcoin Whitepaper

Satoshi Nakamoto publishes "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System" to a cryptography mailing list.

Oct 2008

Genesis Block

The first Bitcoin block is mined, marking the birth of the Bitcoin blockchain and cryptocurrency.

Jan 2009

First Transaction

Laszlo Hanyecz purchases two pizzas for 10,000 BTC, marking the first real-world Bitcoin transaction.

May 2010

First Halving

Bitcoin's first halving event reduces the block reward from 50 BTC to 25 BTC per block.

Nov 2012

Mainstream Adoption

Major companies like Tesla and Square add Bitcoin to their balance sheets, signaling institutional adoption.
